3D Cappilary Vessels
in VR



Developing a therapy to repair or replace damaged heart tissue rather than replace a whole heart, could be invaluable for bettering patient outcomes. Developing such a therapy requires engineering 3D heart tissues in a lab that reflect the nature of the native heart tissues. One of the major roadblocks to engineering tissues is inducing vascularization. Induction of blood vessels into the tissue, or Vascularization, is vital to the survival of the tissue. If blood vessels do not distribute evenly and quickly throughout the tissue, then the tissue will die due to lack of blood flow. Investigating how vascularization occurs in these heart constructs in vivo is important for developing viable engineered heart tissue. This particular dataset is a Micro-CT image stack taken from a casted blood vessels of a mouse heart from the Coulombe lab at Brown University. Micro-CT imaging allows for visualizing the morphology of new vesselformation in animal models and elucidates the efficacy of various molecular engineering techniques in facilitating heart muscle tissue replacement. The whole-heart vessel maps hold information about what kind of vessels, where vessels originate from, and how they integrate into the graft. Viewing these small vessels in an immersive virtual reality setting allows for full resolution of the capillary integration.

This project was built using kitware's paraview VR module.
