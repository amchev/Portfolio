Thesis Project: Computational Methods for Evaluating FUS Protein Mutation Pathology

There are a number of mutations within FUS that have been linked to disease, but the mechanism of the FUS protein’s involvement in disease development, as well as the abundance of the genetic familial studies, casts doubt on many of these mutations. Specifically, a number of point mutations and point deletions have been identified through genetic surveys. The lack of familial genetic information as well as the unstructured nature of the FUS protein leaves the pertinence of mutations undetermined. “Human pathogenic amino acid substitutions tend to occur at less variable sites of proteins. [7]” This project focused on creating a model extrapolated from this evolutionary idea where identifying areas of low evolutionaryvariation, can identify human pathogenic substitions.
